💰 Bond Markets

All eyes are on UK gilts which rose sharply after Rachel Reeves, the UK Chancellor of the Exchequer, brought out the UK budget on Wednesday 30th Oct. It proses over 40B in taxes and 70B in spending. The market interpreted this as inflationary and pared back BoE rate cuts because the inflation would depress growth. As a result, the Sterling dropped 1% as UK bonds slid.

⛽ Commodities

Here is this week’s performance in commodities.

The little box at the bottom left is gold. Like crypto, it also rallied before changing course mid-week to close slightly lower. Silver followed a similar trajectory but dropped further. Oil ended the week higher after being down 6% at the start of the week. The initial drop came after Israel attacked Iran but it was reported that they only attacked military targets hence no disruptions to Iran’s oil industry. Oil was further boosted by OPEC+ who said they could delay planned oil hike scheduled for December by a month or more. 🇺🇸 US

On Tuesday we had the CB Consumer Confidence report that saw an increase to 108.7 vs 99.5 forecasted and 99.2 prior. JOLTS Job Openings dropped to 7.44M vs 7.98M forecast and 7.86M prior.

On Wednesday the ADP Non-farm payrolls report was released and showed an increase of 233K vs 110K forecast and 159K prior. The US Q3 GDP rose 2.8% versus 3% forecasted and 3% prior.

On Thursday, the core PCE m/m came in as expected—0.3% vs 0.3% forecasted and 0.2% prior. Employment cost index q/q dropped to 0.8% from 0.9% versus an expected 0.9%. Unemployment Claims rose stood dropped to 216K from 228K. The forecast was 229K. That day we also had Chicago PMI that dropped to 41.6 versus 46.9 forecast and 46.6 prior.

On Friday we had employment figures and ISM. Average hourly earnings m/m rose to 0.4% versus 0.3% forecast and 0.3% prior. NFPs came in way below expectation at 12K versus 106K forecast and 223K previous. The unemployment was unchanged at 4.1% which was also the expected figure, and ISM manufacturing was 46.5 versus 47.6 forecasted and 47.2 prior.

📁 Docket

I came across a paper that showed that investors, regardless of their trading behavior in other assets, tend to follow momentum-like strategies in crypto. I have written about why crypto is so volatile where I talked about some behavioral aspects among crypto investors that lead to the pump and dump cycles in crypto and volatility spillovers from major to minor coins. From this paper I gather that the behaviors I talked about are not specific to crypto traders but any trader who goes into crypto. I guess the point is that sentiment and momentum work in crypto so anyone who dabbles must use those strategies.
