There’s a short-story by Ian Fleming called “Quantum of Solace”, same guy who wrote the James Bond novels, and no, it’s not another Bond story. It’s about a married couple.

I’m not married, neither am I in a relationship. I like two girls, but we are just talking and figuring things out. So far I’ve figured that there’s one I would marry instantly even right now, but it would be the hard relationship. The ones with the most painful moments, I won’t say why. The other one and I are like two sides of the same coin. If I were a woman, I’d be her. But something hitherto unnamed stops us from becoming a couple. I didn’t want to be in this position, both relationships developed while the other one was cold, but here I am.

Like anything in life, a marriage is ups and downs, but there’s a time in every marriage when things are at their most toxic. That’s when things are said at each new fight that are overshadow the previous thing said, which was equally unforgivable. I presume that this is when any cheating happens. Some couples divorce at this point, sometimes one kills the other, and other times some compromises are made that maintain the relationship in name only; under the surface it is as if they were divorced already. But some couples make it through. It is estimated that about 40% of marriages end in divorce (the rate differs by country and over time). So you could say that overall, more couples make it than those that don’t.

Those that do find true love in one another.

Its always darkest before the dawn, as they say. I’m not married so I really don’t know what happens in a marriage. I was also raised by a single mum, who’s few relationships often ended in absolute agony. I really have no concept of what a healthy relationship is. This idea that the moment you find love is actually way into a marriage is interesting to me because all the romcoms and popular beliefs about love and relationships had me believing that you find true love when you meet your person. And you are supposed to know when you meet your person, hence why I’m surprised to find myself in love with two people.

Quantum of solace gave me an insight into why some couples make it and some don’t. Quantum of solace is the idea that even in a married couple who absolutely despise each other, there should be a modicum of love. When it’s not there, that couple is doomed. The way to know is in how you fight with each other. There are things that are out of bounds no matter what, and if one of you breaches these bounds, its time to part ways for good. You can apply this concept to all your other relationships. One day I will tell you about how I applied it to my toxic family.

Anyway, what’s keeping me up at night is that I really don’t want to love two girls. I have to choose. It is said that who you marry is the biggest decision you will make in your life, and there’s a very high chance that whoever I choose will end up being my wife. I have a very traditional view of marriage meaning that divorce is out of the question for me, but I’d do it if my partner really wanted it.
