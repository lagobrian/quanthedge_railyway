Why do I think this is a good idea?

There are many different ways to analyze and trade the markets. Most profitable traders use a hybrid approach. Market commentary can help you understand macro and fundamentals; sentiment analysis can help you identify trends and pick market turns; positioning can help you find imbalances and potential reversals, or identify trends; correlations can help you find sympathetic trades; and technical analysis can help you enter, exit trades and manage risk. What I find missing from these is a kind of quantitative analysis that can give further insights and help manage risk. You always see these econometric papers that are written posthumously about a certain thing i.e. what asset provided the best diversification benefits during COVID etc (how is such information supposed to help anyone in future?), but when the event was ongoing, no one was crunching any numbers and talking about it. The best you can get at such times is some chart on twitter that is being passed around. This isn’t reliable and you may get the info too late.

Another reason for doing this is to have skin in the game. This publication does a few things: it puts under a microscope the various speculations and conjectures about markets, it looks for stories hidden behind data, it is a water hole for quants and wannabe quants, and most importantly, it looks for edge and hedge benefits. After doing some studies and writing about them, I’ve realized that what is missing in these works is relevance. An easy fix to that is to put all the things I write about to work and then over time we’ll see what is helpful and what isn’t.

I find that there is a disconnect between quantitative and econometric research, and real-life trading. There are a lot of papers and research about markets but I don’t see a lot of those models being used in real life. The thing about quantitative analysis is that it tells a story and a good way to use it in trading is to bring to light the different prevailing stories in the markets at the present. So while most of the time I will be researching something big, at the end of the week I will publish this report and anything good from the research will be included in the weekly market report.
