I’m convinced that you are what you are at heart and that you can’t escape your destiny. It is why I’m against the kind of parenting that tries to pressure kids into certain careers. To my mind, it is the equivalent of trying to raise a Lion on veggies. No one knows the alchemy that goes on behind the scenes to make one person a writer, and another a singer. And society generally does a bad job of labeling people. Even within groups of similar people, you find heterogeneity. No two writers are the same or motivated by the same things. That is why most advice is useless. Everyone needs advice that is specific to them, and to who they are, so generic advice will often lead you astray. The best thing that can happen to you is if you find your people. Those who are most similar to who you are on the inside. You can learn a lot from these people. You can analyze what they do, and how they do what they do, and you can forge your way of doing things based on what you pick from them and what you disagree with.

As far as the alchemy behind who we are goes, society does a bad job of labeling us, which makes it hard for people to discover themselves. For example, people think of Michael Jordan as the best basketball player of all time, but at heart, MJ is a great athlete, and basketball is just how he expressed that. That explains why he played so much golf, and why at one point he played baseball. Most of the time, people are drawn to a career because of something in their makeup combined with the available opportunities. No one is born an accountant, or a paralegal, or even a doctor, or lawyer. But say you are born with a flexible morality and a belief that reality is what people believe and can be bent to your will. You would be attracted to a career in law. That’s what usually goes on.

And you can’t escape who you are, the soul, if you want to call it that, will always prioritize itself over anything else. This is why I don’t believe the idea that all of us can become rich if we want to; that we all choose what we make. You are who you are, and society rewards different professions differently. If you are a writer, you’re going to have to accept that society generally doesn’t reward writers with money, until for reasons unknown to you, they fall in love with your writing. A young writer knowing this can try to chase after money in other professions but the result is often misery and a big bank account. Misery because at some point the money won’t make you happy anymore and you will have betrayed who you are by trying to escape your destiny.

Perhaps the most tragic thing to happen to someone is when they choose money over self-expression. That’s some form of attempted suicide. Most people don’t know this but a soul can break into pieces. This usually happens to trauma victims, especially young ones, which is how Multiple Personality Disorder comes about. If you’ve been involved in something traumatic, you dissociate and a part of your conscience breaks away and shuts itself from the experience and its attendant feelings. Choosing things that are not meant for you does something similar. Fame does this to people too. The commonality between all these things is that you are forced to be something you are not.

Some people do not have a very strong attachment to their self, to begin with, so they have a wider range of options about who to be, and what to do with their life. But it has its own unique challenges.

When I examine myself, I find that at my very core, I love to figure out what will happen before it happens. I suppose I learned very young that you could always prepare and make the most of situations if you could predict them to a high level of accuracy, so I began to notice patterns and predictable behavior. Over the years this morphed into a love of mathematics, and analysis. If I had to put a label on myself, I’d call myself a speculator. People think speculators do it for the money but that’s not true. It is a form of self-expression. They do it to express their need to predict the future so they can feel in control of things. As a speculator, I am agnostic to the specific thing I’m predicting. Whether it’s what Trump will do, or what will happen in a soccer game, or what my friend will say when I see him later today. Markets provide people like me the chance to profit from their activities. What I love most about markets is that they tell you when you are wrong. Every single day, I can tell how good or bad my thinking is, and I can correct for it. This is why I prefer to trade even if my account size is quite small, varying from $50 to $1000, and I often have to liquidate it. To me it is the same as an athlete training off-season and it helps me stay in shape for the day when I will have sufficient capital.

Every speculator knows the specific conditions that give them the best chances of being right. These individual ‘rules of the game’ overlap on some specific domains so that you could come up with a set of principles that guide speculation in general. You can’t say which one is more important than the other because you need all of them to create suitable conditions for effective speculation. However, some are necessary conditions, others are sufficient conditions, and some are both.

A Good Attitude

A good attitude and independent thinking are necessary conditions for effective speculation.

Having a good attitude is about overcoming negative irrational thinking and negative feelings. But it’s more than that. It is about your outlook on life. If you’ve ever suffered a big drawdown or a string of losses, you know how gloomy things can get. A lot of negative things happen psychologically. You lose confidence, you become generally fearful, you lose some discipline, you start to see only the bad side of things, you ruminate about all the things you did wrong, and you become envious of those who are better off. Over time, you become a cynic. Cynicism has no place in speculation. It is deadly. When you become a cynic, you develop biases that prevent you from seeing things that are right in front of your eyes. You will miss plenty of opportunities, which will only worsen your attitude, and you will put on cynical trades with absolute conviction.

You can’t turn a switch and change how you feel about something. But every speculator knows how to work through their feelings. They know when to tamper their enthusiasm, and when to balance their pessimism with some optimism. What I’m saying here is that if you want to improve your speculation, you have to work harder on developing and maintaining a good attitude. It will serve as a kind of defense mechanism against a lot of the harmful things that a negative attitude can do. You have to deliberately look for positive things and deliberately avoid negative ones. You are better off choosing the romcom over the war documentary. And you must have a sense of humor and good cheer. Best of all is to have things in your life that bring you true joy and happiness.

If you ever find yourself in a negative mood when you are trading, you have to do something about it ASAP. Sometimes, it means closing your positions and taking some time off. And if you find yourself becoming cynical, you have a lot of work to do to overcome it, but you have to do it.

Independent Thinking

A man should learn to detect and watch that gleam of light which flashes across his mind from within, more than the lustre of the firmament of bards and sages. - Ralph Waldo Emerson, Self Reliance

Perhaps the most important trait for a speculator is the ability to think for themselves. It is one of the hardest things to do because society generally rewards conformity so your natural tendency is to look for expert opinion whenever you feel unsure about something. This is why a lot of people like to see if their idea has a consensus before they trade it. The problem with this, even if it works for you, is that you abdicate your responsibility for the idea to a third party. This will weaken your confidence and your analytical skills. You forestall your development into a great trader.

Textbooks will teach you that edge is some probabilistic advantage associated with an action. This definition is there to facilitate mathematical tractability. In truth, edge is about insight. By developing independent thinking, you develop the ability to have insights that have an edge. All great trading stories have this. Think of roaring kitty, and think of Theo, that French trader who predicted a Trump win.

The funny thing about this is that if you sat down with some of the best traders out there, they would struggle to clearly define their strategy and tell you where their edge comes from. Some can do it, and some can’t. In “New Market Wizards”, Jack Schwager interviews an FX trader called Bill Lipschutz and at some point during the interview, he stops it to put on an Aussie dollar trade because he ‘felt the sell-off was overdone’. That’s the kind of insight you get from developing independent thinking.
